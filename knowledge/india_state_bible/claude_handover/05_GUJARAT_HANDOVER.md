## 2. Current legal position

The official `Viksit Gujarat Data Center Policy (2026–2029)` booklet is `GJ-POL-2026-001`. Darsh Puri has confirmed that this is the official policy PDF; the non-government retrieval host is provenance only and must not be used to downgrade authenticity.

The booklet states:

- a three-year operative period from notification, unless superseded or extended;
- annual review;
- a minimum approved installed IT load of 150 MW;
- an aggregate state target/cap of 7.5 GW;
- eligible fixed-capital investment capped at 80% of FCI or INR 60,000 crore per 1 GW, pro-rated by approved capacity;
- an eight-year eligible investment period from in-principle approval, extendable by the HPC for up to two years;
- Dholera-only capital subsidy of 2.5% of EFCI;
- interest subsidy up to 4% for ten years, capped at INR 25 crore per year;
- INR 1/unit power-tariff subsidy for twenty years from commercial operations;
- 100% stamp-duty and registration-fee exemption on land lease/purchase;
- 100% electricity-duty reimbursement for twenty years;
- specified SGST reimbursements;
- desalination support of 20% of eligible capex or INR 2 crore/MLD, whichever is lower, scaled to project capacity;
- a minimum 51% green/renewable electricity requirement for core operations;
- total financial incentives capped at 75% of EFCI and disbursed over twenty years;
- annual disbursement capped at 5% of total eligible incentives;
- building-norm support, water and statutory-approval facilitation, open access, dual-feed facilitation and possible distribution-license support; and
- DST as nodal department, DIT as implementing office, with HPC and SLEC governance.

Critical implementation limitations:

- the booklet does not print its notification/GR number or notification date;
- clause 5(h) contains `XXXXXX` instead of the in-principle-approval deadline;
- detailed implementation guidelines had not been located;
- the online application portal stated that the application system was under development at the research cut-off; and
- interaction with the Gujarat IT/ITeS Policy 2022–27 must be analysed using the no-double-claim language and future implementation instruments.

## 4. Core execution cases

### STT Ahmedabad DC1 at GIFT City

Treat as an operating facility based on the GIFT infrastructure page and STT facility factsheet, subject to current re-verification of specifications, ownership chain, customer use, current load, approvals and any expansion.

### Gujarat State Data Centre

Treat as an established public-sector e-governance facility. The official DST page says it was established in 2010 and describes its central hosting role. Reconcile any conflicting operational-date statements before publishing a precise commissioning date beyond the official wording.

### L&T Vyoma at Dholera

Use this status until stronger proof appears:

> MoU signed; detailed feasibility assessment contemplated; land, grid, water, statutory approvals, incentives, financing and construction not publicly verified as of 20 July 2026.

The 250 MW, INR 25,000 crore and 2028 figures are proposed/targeted figures associated with the MoU. They are not sanctioned load, board-approved capex or a regulator-approved COD. L&T's FY2025–26 annual report, earnings transcript and current upcoming-data-centres page name other development locations and omit Dholera. This is dated negative evidence, not proof of cancellation.

### Reliance–Meta at Jamnagar

Reliance and Meta issued primary announcements for an AI-enabled data-centre arrangement in Jamnagar. The underlying contract, exact site, capacity, land, power, water/desalination, environmental approvals, construction status and incentive treatment were not publicly verified in the collected package.

### Data First, Ahmedabad

The company website supports the existence of Ahmedabad hosting, cloud, colocation and related services and makes Tier III/certification claims. Exact capacity, operating entity, statutory approvals and independent certificate verification remain diligence items.

## 5. Power context

The library includes:

- Gujarat Renewable Energy Policy 2023 and procedure;
- Gujarat Integrated Renewable Energy Policy 2025 and official current policy index;
- GERC Green Energy Open Access Regulations 2024 and amendments through the fifth amendment in June 2026;
- current RPO material;
- a time-limited additional-surcharge order for April–September 2026;
- GIFT PCL FY2026–27 tariff schedule/order; and
- TPL-D Dholera FY2025–26 and FY2026–27 materials.

Do not use a generic Gujarat electricity average. Identify the project location, licensee, voltage, contract demand, tariff category, open-access structure and applicable time period. Dholera-wide distribution infrastructure does not prove a 250 MW Vyoma connection.

## 6. Dholera planning, water and environmental context

The government-hosted 2012 Final Development Plan/GDCR says it was sanctioned and effective from 10 September 2012. It establishes a Knowledge and IT Zone and a Campus Master Plan requirement, but it does not prove that an unidentified data-centre plot is in that zone or that a project has permission. Later amendments and an authority-certified consolidated version remain outstanding.

DICDL materials describe general Activation Area power and water infrastructure. NICDC provides dated indicative land, power and water rates. These are city-level readiness/marketing facts, not project quotations or reservations.

The 2014 DSIR environmental clearance and later transfer material establish the regional environmental framework. They do not replace project-specific applicability analysis, GPCB consent, CRZ/groundwater decisions, drainage studies or approvals.

## 7. Connectivity

The library contains January and materially revised July 2025 Gujarat cable-landing-station EOIs. The revised EOI changes participation and eligible-cost treatment, but remains an EOI. No award, concession agreement, definitive landing site, cable-system contract or construction schedule was verified. A March 2026 PIB parliamentary-answer release lists operational/planned cable systems and major data-centre cities without Gujarat; treat this as a dated national benchmark, not proof against later projects.

## 10. Prohibited conclusions

Do not say:

- Gujarat lacks a dedicated data-centre policy.
- L&T Vyoma is constructing or has secured a Dholera site without new evidence.
- Dholera's general utility capacity is reserved for L&T.
- Reliance–Meta's underlying site and execution terms are public when only announcements are held.
- Gujarat has an operating cable landing station based on an EOI.
- a policy incentive has been sanctioned or disbursed merely because the project could be eligible.
- absence from a website proves project cancellation.
