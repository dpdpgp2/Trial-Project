## 00:00:00–00:02:37 — Welcome and Felicitation of Dignitaries

The master of ceremonies introduces the senior officials present on stage, including Chief Secretary M. K. Das; Additional Chief Secretary, Finance, Dr. T. Natarajan; Additional Chief Secretary, Industries and Mines, Mamta Verma; and Secretary, Science and Technology, P. Bharathi.

The audience is invited to welcome the dignitaries with applause.

## 00:02:38–00:05:53 — Welcome Address by P. Bharathi

Hon’ble Chief Minister Bhupendra Patel, Hon’ble Deputy Chief Minister Harsh Sanghavi, Hon’ble Minister for Science and Technology Arjun Modhwadia, respected Chief Secretary Manoj Kumar Das, Additional Chief Secretary for Industries Mamta Verma, Additional Chief Secretary for Finance Dr. T. Natarajan, senior government officials, distinguished industry leaders, investors, technology experts, members of the media, ladies and gentlemen—a very warm good morning to all of you.

It is my privilege to welcome you to the launch of the **Viksit Gujarat Data Centre Policy**.

It is often said that data is the new oil, but data is even more valuable than oil. Every digital payment, artificial-intelligence model, cloud application, online transaction, government service, and innovation is powered by data. Data creates value only when it is stored, processed, secured, and made available through data centres. That is why data centres have emerged as strategic digital infrastructure for every developed economy and are becoming as important as physical infrastructure.

India’s digital-infrastructure journey is gaining remarkable momentum, and Gujarat intends to accelerate that momentum through today’s policy launch.

Globally, there are around 12,000 data centres, with the United States and China together accounting for about 70 percent of capacity. India generates approximately 20 percent of the world’s data but has only around 3 percent of global data-centre capacity. This represents a tremendous opportunity.

Demand for data-centre capacity in India is expected to grow rapidly because of artificial intelligence, cloud computing, digital public infrastructure, financial services, and related sectors. The key question is: where will India’s future digital infrastructure be built? Gujarat is ready to answer that question.

International connectivity is another important development. Gujarat has two planned cable landing stations, and another cable-landing-station project is expected to be announced soon. This will significantly strengthen Gujarat’s digital connectivity and make it more attractive as a data-centre destination.

Today’s policy is not merely an incentive policy; it is a roadmap for building India’s future digital infrastructure. It will contribute to the visions of Digital India, Atmanirbhar Bharat, and a developed Gujarat with a projected economy of USD 3.5 trillion by 2047.

I express my sincere gratitude to the Chief Minister, Deputy Chief Minister, Minister for Science and Technology, Chief Secretary, Principal Secretaries, Additional Chief Secretaries for Industries and Finance, and all others for their continuous guidance and unwavering support.

Every industrial revolution has been driven by a defining infrastructure. The last century was driven by highways, ports, and power plants. This century will be driven by digital infrastructure, with data centres at the heart of that transformation.

Let us work together to make Gujarat the data-centre capital of India and one of the world’s leading destinations for hyperscale digital infrastructure.

Thank you once again.

## 00:05:54–00:08:22 — Policy Unveiling

The master of ceremonies thanks P. Bharathi for her welcome and opening remarks.

She states that the launch marks a monumental milestone in Gujarat’s journey toward technological leadership. As India develops into a digital powerhouse, data has emerged as the backbone of economic growth and innovation.

India generates nearly 20 percent of the world’s data, but only a small share is hosted domestically. Storing data within the country is therefore described as a national priority for strengthening digital security and data sovereignty.

Gujarat is presented as well positioned to lead this transformation because of its industrial ecosystem, global connectivity, and hubs such as GIFT City and Dholera Special Investment Region, which offer a plug-and-play foundation for hyperscale investment.

Under the leadership of Prime Minister Narendra Modi and Chief Minister Bhupendra Patel, the Government of Gujarat formally launches the **Viksit Gujarat Data Centre Policy 2026–2029**.

The Chief Minister and all dignitaries on stage are invited to unveil the official policy booklet. The unveiling is welcomed with applause.

The master of ceremonies says that this first-of-its-kind policy sets the stage for Gujarat to become a global destination for green artificial intelligence and hyperscale data centres. The audience is then invited to watch a special audio-visual presentation.

## 00:08:23–00:12:34 — Audio-Visual Presentation on the Policy

Across the world, data has become a foundation of modern economies. India generates nearly one-fifth of the world’s data but hosts only a small fraction of global data-centre capacity.

The technological revolution requires a place that can build the infrastructure to power it. Gujarat is presented as that place.

From manufacturing and pharmaceuticals to advanced electronics and semiconductors, Gujarat has built industries that drive India forward. It is now ready to support an even larger digital transformation.

India is developing a resilient ecosystem spanning semiconductor and chip manufacturing, quantum computing, secure data centres, a strong information-technology backbone, and a dynamic start-up ecosystem. Together, these capabilities can make India a natural hub for affordable, scalable, and secure artificial-intelligence solutions.

Under Prime Minister Narendra Modi’s leadership and Chief Minister Bhupendra Patel’s guidance, Gujarat introduces the **Viksit Gujarat Data Centre Policy 2026–2029**, described as India’s first dedicated policy of its kind for building a world-class ecosystem for green-AI and hyperscale data centres.

By recognising data centres as strategic infrastructure, Gujarat aims to provide the reliability, resilience, and long-term certainty demanded by global investors. Aligned with Digital India, the policy places Gujarat at the forefront of India’s digital transformation.

The policy offers a comprehensive package of fiscal incentives, including capital and interest subsidies, power-tariff support, SGST reimbursement, electricity-duty reimbursement, support for desalination plants, stamp-duty exemptions, and non-fiscal incentives.

The policy is intended to accelerate investment, enable hyperscale artificial-intelligence infrastructure, strengthen India’s digital sovereignty, and establish Gujarat as a leading national data-centre destination.

Gujarat has an installed power base of approximately 69 gigawatts, including around 47 gigawatts of renewable energy, described as the highest in India. This provides the energy backbone required for the artificial-intelligence era.

Dholera, India’s first greenfield smart industrial city, and GIFT City, India’s international financial services centre, are presented as an integrated ecosystem in which digital infrastructure, industry, and global finance can come together.

Global data-centre capacity is projected in the presentation to approach 200 gigawatts by the end of the decade. Gujarat aims to lead the next wave of digital infrastructure.

As Gujarat advances toward a USD 3.5 trillion economy by 2047, it is expected to play a defining role in the vision of Viksit Bharat. The future will not simply be digital; it will be intelligent, sustainable, and secure—and Gujarat is building the foundation.

## 00:12:35–00:17:35 — Address by Chief Secretary M. K. Das

The Chief Secretary greets the Chief Minister, Deputy Chief Minister, Minister for Science and Technology, senior members of Team Gujarat, officials, guests, and industry leaders.

He says he is very pleased to attend the launch of the data-centre policy. While people may say that the twenty-first century will be defined by technology, he believes that growth will come largely from digital infrastructure, with data at its core.

He recalls that people of his generation who studied computer science in the late 1980s and early 1990s learned about databases, database-management systems, artificial intelligence, and machine learning, but hyperscale data centres were not yet widely understood.

India now generates a huge volume of data and has one of the fastest-growing digital economies in the world. Although India accounts for roughly 20 percent of data generated globally, it has only around 3 percent of data-centre capacity.

He congratulates Team Gujarat for producing a bold, forward-looking policy. He says Gujarat has progressed significantly under the Chief Minister’s leadership and has moved from a lower-income position toward a higher middle-income trajectory.

Gujarat, he says, is the first state to introduce such a futuristic policy. Whether in semiconductors, data centres, advanced manufacturing, space, or other emerging sectors, Gujarat is taking a strong lead.

Data centres require substantial water and power. A one-gigawatt data-centre cluster may require power comparable to that used by millions of homes, and large quantities of water. Gujarat, however, has the advantages of the sea, sunlight, wind, and a strong renewable-energy potential. The state is already moving toward a renewable-power target of approximately 100 gigawatts.

The government continuously considers new sector-specific policies to stimulate growth. The Chief Minister regularly asks departments to act quickly on measures needed for Gujarat’s development.

India is described as the fastest-growing major country and Gujarat as one of its fastest-growing regions. Investors are encouraged to take full advantage of the policy.

The Chief Secretary states that the government expects approximately ₹6 lakh crore in investment, several gigawatts of data-storage capacity, and substantial employment generation.

He again congratulates Team Gujarat and concludes with “Jai Hind.”

## 00:17:36–00:25:18 — Address by Minister for Science and Technology Arjun Modhwadia

The master of ceremonies thanks the Chief Secretary and invites Minister for Science and Technology Arjun Modhwadia to address the gathering.

The Minister greets the Chief Minister, Deputy Chief Minister, Chief Secretary, secretaries, and industry representatives.

He recalls that India once accounted for nearly 25 percent of global GDP, but colonial rule damaged the country’s economy and skills, leaving India among the poorest countries in the world.

India missed much of the Industrial Revolution because it was exploited under colonialism. During the computer and information-technology revolutions, India eventually caught up, although Gujarat initially did not benefit as much as it could have.

He says that under Prime Minister Narendra Modi, India has moved rapidly in areas such as vaccine manufacturing and semiconductors, not merely catching up but increasingly becoming a leader.

The present era is one of artificial intelligence and quantum computing, and India must advance as quickly as possible. Data is described as the “new oil well” of a developing economy.

With the support of the Prime Minister, the Chief Minister has decided that Gujarat should become a prime mover in this emerging field. The Minister says he is pleased that the policy has now been announced.

India still has a relatively small data-centre presence. He cites approximately 5,500 data centres in the United States, more than 500 in the United Kingdom, and only about 200 in India.

He also compares capacity, saying the United States has around 30 gigawatts, while India’s operational and pipeline capacity remains much smaller. He then states that Gujarat has received proposals totalling around 10 gigawatts.

These projects are not merely data centres; they are part of the wider artificial-intelligence ecosystem. If Gujarat becomes a leader in data centres, it can become a leader not only in India but globally in the broader AI ecosystem.

He says proposals of around 10 gigawatts have been received and that the plan is to develop approximately 8 gigawatts, enabling Gujarat to build an ecosystem comparable in scale to the rest of the country.

The Minister praises the Chief Minister’s determination in approving the policy and the Deputy Chief Minister’s contribution to developing it. He notes that finance departments are often cautious about approving incentive-heavy policies, but the proposals placed before the Finance Department were approved.

He calls the launch a historic moment for Gujarat. India is fortunate, he says, to have a visionary leader in Prime Minister Narendra Modi, who is working to restore India’s earlier stature and establish it among the world’s most developed countries.

Under Chief Minister Bhupendra Patel, Gujarat aims to become a hub for the AI ecosystem over the next decade. Industry cooperation will be essential.

He invites investors to come to Gujarat, invest in the sector, and become part of this revolution, assuring them of the government’s support.

He concludes with “Bharat Mata ki Jai.”

## 00:25:19–00:39:15 — Address by Deputy Chief Minister Harsh Sanghavi

The master of ceremonies thanks the Minister and invites Deputy Chief Minister Harsh Sanghavi to speak.

The Deputy Chief Minister welcomes Chief Minister Bhupendra Patel, Minister Arjun Modhwadia, Chief Secretary Manoj Kumar Das, Additional Chief Secretary for Finance Dr. T. Natarajan, Mamta Verma, P. Bharathi, and investors from across India and abroad. He particularly congratulates representatives connected with hyperscale computing and artificial intelligence on the new opportunities being created.

He recalls earlier phases of India’s semiconductor ambitions. A decision concerning semiconductors was taken in 1976, but by 1990 the mission had failed. A major semiconductor-park announcement was again made in Hyderabad between 2006 and 2009, but the project did not materialise, and the site was eventually converted for real-estate use.

He contrasts this with the semiconductor mission launched under Prime Minister Narendra Modi in 2021. By 2025, multiple semiconductor projects, fabrication initiatives, and OSAT facilities had begun progressing. He says Gujarat demonstrated its execution capacity by inaugurating more than three semiconductor-related facilities within four months.

He congratulates Team Gujarat for successfully moving important projects from announcement to implementation.

Under Chief Minister Bhupendra Patel’s guidance, Gujarat has now launched the Viksit Gujarat Data Centre Policy. Other states have discussed data centres for years, but Gujarat is described as the first state to launch an official, dedicated data-centre policy of this nature.

The policy is designed for around 7 gigawatts of capacity. The earlier figures mentioned for Dholera alone were already significant, and the state has received request letters from leading Indian and global companies seeking to establish data centres across Gujarat with demand approximately twice the capacity covered by the policy.

This demonstrates investor confidence in the state government. Even before the policy was formally launched, companies were making Gujarat their first choice.

“Gujarat is a brand. Gujarat is a guarantee. Gujarat does what it says.” The Deputy Chief Minister says investors in India and abroad have complete confidence in this proposition.

He then explains why Gujarat is becoming a preferred data-centre destination: it has a stable government, policy-driven governance, reliable electricity, water availability, and an established industrial ecosystem.

Data centres require uninterrupted electricity and large quantities of water. Gujarat has successfully provided seamless electricity supply to industrial areas across the state. The state also intends to ensure reliable water supply, while incorporating sustainability into both energy and water use.

He clarifies that water for data centres will not be diverted from farmers or other industries. The policy requires that at least 51 percent of the energy used by data centres come from green-energy sources.

The policy also gives special emphasis to desalination so that seawater can be converted into usable freshwater.

He states with pride that Dholera is expected to become the world’s largest data-centre city by capacity. The state government has prepared the infrastructure required for the establishment of data centres under the Chief Minister’s guidance.

A stakeholder-consultation meeting will be convened shortly. Investors will be shown available land parcels. Companies ready to move ahead with investments in Dholera will be assigned senior nodal officers to support their applications on a day-to-day basis and prevent unnecessary delays across government departments.

The Deputy Chief Minister invites investors to Dholera, describing it as an emerging international city with a rapidly developing ecosystem for manufacturing and other activities. Dholera’s airport is expected to begin operations soon, and a semi-high-speed train is planned between Ahmedabad and Dholera. Other infrastructure projects are also progressing rapidly.

The government is not only seeking data centres in Dholera. It is also working toward making Dholera a headquarters location for global capability centres.

The opportunity is described as exceptional. The policy covers limited capacity, while demand is already about twice as high. Those who wish to benefit from it are invited to invest in Gujarat.

He warns that anyone who “misses the Gujarat train” may regret it later.

The Finance, Science and Technology, and Energy Departments have worked intensely on the policy, often late into the night. Their work may not appear in newspaper headlines, but it has made the policy possible. He congratulates Team Gujarat and reiterates the government’s commitment to working with investors for the state’s development.

He concludes by thanking the audience.

## 00:39:16–00:46:35 — Address by Chief Minister Bhupendra Patel

The master of ceremonies thanks Deputy Chief Minister Harsh Sanghavi and invites Chief Minister Bhupendra Patel to deliver the concluding special address.

The Chief Minister greets Deputy Chief Minister Harsh Sanghavi, Minister Arjun Modhwadia, Chief Secretary M. K. Das, Additional Chief Secretary Dr. T. Natarajan, Additional Chief Secretary Mamta Verma, Secretary P. Bharathi, invited dignitaries, and members of the media.

Referring to the Deputy Chief Minister’s remarks, he says credit for Gujarat’s progress belongs to the entire Team Gujarat. The Prime Minister’s guiding principle—“Sabka Saath, Sabka Vikas, Sabka Vishwas, Sabka Prayas”—is reflected in the way all stakeholders work with mutual trust.

The event is presented as an example of the transformative results that visionary leadership can achieve under Prime Minister Narendra Modi.

Science and technology are essential to the economic strength of any society. During his years in government, Prime Minister Modi did not confine science and technology to laboratories; he made them central pillars of national development.

By thinking ahead of the times and bringing together innovation and technology, India is developing robust digital infrastructure for Viksit Bharat.

Data is the foundation of the digital system and is becoming a basis of India’s economic growth. The growth of the digital economy, artificial intelligence, cloud computing, electronics manufacturing, and other emerging sectors is generating new momentum.

Under the Prime Minister’s leadership, India has become one of the world’s largest users and producers of data, creating major opportunities for world-class digital infrastructure.

The Chief Minister refers to measures in the Union Budget intended to make India a global hub for cloud and artificial-intelligence infrastructure, including a long-term tax holiday for global data-centre operations.

Gujarat has consistently worked to translate the Prime Minister’s ideas into reality. The state is emerging as an important player in India’s digital ecosystem.

As artificial intelligence and related technologies expand, demand for scalable data-storage infrastructure will also grow. To meet future data-centre demand and strengthen the state’s digital economy, Gujarat has announced the Data Centre Policy.

The Chief Minister congratulates everyone involved in preparing the policy.

The policy will support cloud services, artificial intelligence, digital transactions, e-commerce platforms, smart-manufacturing systems, digital governance, and related services.

He expresses confidence that Gujarat will become India’s best destination for data centres.

Gujarat offers reliable electricity, water, high-quality roads, and world-class infrastructure. GIFT City has established itself as India’s fintech capital. The GCC policy is encouraging global capability centres, while the AI Centre of Excellence at GIFT City provides a platform bringing together businesses, start-ups, and experts to develop new solutions.

Sanand and Dholera are advancing toward becoming major semiconductor hubs. These strengths make Gujarat a natural choice for data centres.

The state’s objective is “zero effect” on the environment and “zero defect” in production. The new data-centre policy gives equal importance to technological excellence and environmental responsibility and encourages investments aligned with both.

The policy reflects the state government’s commitment to long-term, sustainable development.

The Gujarat Data Centre Policy will accelerate not only Gujarat’s growth but also India’s future development. It will support a new generation of digital infrastructure.

The Chief Minister invites investors and industry leaders to become part of Gujarat’s growth story and contribute to the journey from Viksit Gujarat to Viksit Bharat.

Gujarat is also taking a lead in helping India become a global soft-power leader and a knowledge-based economy. In the coming years, new technology and innovation will become the backbone of the state’s economy.

He concludes with the hope that all stakeholders will contribute to making Gujarat the technology engine of India.

“Bharat Mata ki Jai. Vande Mataram. Jai Jai Garvi Gujarat.”

## 00:46:36–00:47:26 — Closing Remarks

The master of ceremonies thanks Chief Minister Bhupendra Patel for his inspiring guidance and invites the audience to applaud him once again.

As the historic session draws to a close, gratitude is expressed to the Chief Minister, whose vision and commitment are said to be advancing Gujarat toward a technological revolution; to Deputy Chief Minister Harsh Sanghavi; to Minister Arjun Modhwadia; and to all senior officials on stage for their inspiring presence.

The global technology partners, industry leaders, and guests are thanked for partnering in India’s digital destiny.

“Thank you very much. Many thanks.”
